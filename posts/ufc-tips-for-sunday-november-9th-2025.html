UFC ON ESPN 73 — Full Card Tips

All odds supplied by Teambet

Christian Leroy Duncan vs Marco Tulio

Play: Marco Tulio to Win — $1.53
Tulio has shown real finishing power and composure in his UFC run, stopping both opponents by strikes. Duncan is creative but inconsistent and can be pressured when fights get physical. Tulio’s sturdier boxing and forward pressure should let him dictate the exchanges and score the cleaner moments over three rounds.

Chris Padilla vs Ismael Bonfim

Play: Chris Padilla to Win — $2.64
Padilla has been steady, disciplined and continues to show improvement, especially defensively. Bonfim is dangerous but chaotic — when his early speed doesn't break opponents, he fades. Over three rounds, Padilla’s composure and grappling edge give him a strong chance to win minutes and steal rounds.

Matt Schnell vs Joseph Morales

Play: Fight NOT to Go the Distance — $1.48
Both men are aggressive finishers and neither is known for sustained durability. Schnell is explosive but hittable, while Morales attacks relentlessly once he gains control. Given their styles and finishing histories, this fight strongly trends toward an early stoppage either way.

Ricky Simon vs Raoni Barcelos

Play: Ricky Simon to Win — $1.57
Simon’s wrestling pressure and pace are difference-makers. Even if takedowns don’t stick, constant attempts will force Barcelos backward and limit his ability to counter. With Simon also improving his boxing volume, he should be able to win rounds through control and output.

Muslim Salikhov vs Uros Medic

Play: Uros Medic to Win — $1.62
Medic is the younger, faster, and more accurate striker at this stage. Salikhov is still dangerous, but at 41 his reactions have slowed and he gets hit more often. If Medic keeps this at range, his southpaw accuracy and volume should be decisive.

Gabriel Bonfim vs Randy Brown

Play: Gabriel Bonfim to Win — $1.53
Bonfim’s clearest path is wrestling and submission pressure. Brown is long and sharp on the feet, but his takedown defense has been exploited lately, and Bonfim’s grappling output is high. If Bonfim commits to early takedowns, he should control position and find finishing chances.
